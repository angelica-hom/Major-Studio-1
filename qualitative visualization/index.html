Worth a Thousand Words



In the 19th century, recently-freed African Americans used photography as a tool for racial justice to fight against racist misrepresentations and to take control of their self-representation. How did African American men and women use their newfound creative freedom to redefine their identity?

Through the Smithsonian's Open Access initiative, this data visualization allows you to explore a word analysis of the titles of the photography collection at the National Museum of African American History & Culture.
